Main Functions of BMS

  • Tracks voltage, current, and temperature of each battery cell.
  • Monitors the state of charge (SOC) how much battery is left.
  • Estimates the state of health (SOH) battery life & efficiency.
  • Prevents overcharging (which can damage cells).
  • Prevents over-discharging (which can shorten battery life).
  • Avoids overheating or short circuits.
  • Keeps all cells at the same voltage level.
  • Uses techniques like passive balancing (dissipating extra charge) or active balancing (redistributing charge).
  • Sends data to EV controllers, display units, or cloud systems.
  • Uses protocols like CAN bus.
  • Shuts down or isolates the battery during faults.
  • Ensures safe operation under different environmental conditions.

      21. Key Parameters of EV Batteries

    21. Capacity (kWh): Determines how much energy the battery stores (like the fuel tank size).
    22. Voltage (V): Higher voltage allows more efficient power transfer (modern EVs use 400–800V systems).
    23. Energy Density: Energy per unit weight (Wh/kg) or volume (Wh/L).
    24. Cycle Life: Number of charge–discharge cycles before capacity drops (usually 1000–3000+ cycles).
    25. State of Charge (SOC): How full the battery is (100%).
    26. State of Health (SOH): Indicates aging level of the battery.
